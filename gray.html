Gray

Making the Gray area easier

  • Role: UI/UX Designer
  • Tools: Sketch, Invision
  • Duration: Nov 2020 - Dec 2020

The Problem

Recent college grads can often feel lost when entering the workforce. With so many different platforms, job search sites, and recruiting services landing an entry-level dream position can seem like just that, a dream. Taking a step in the right direction can often feel like a “gray area,” where they know they have the skills to succeed, but just don’t know how to get there.

The Solution

Creating a social, yet unintimidating space where users can not only find jobs and apply to them, but can also learn how to network, interview, and find a mentor within their field.

The user

  • Recent College Grads (ages 22-26)
  • Mentors (anyone not in an entry level position)
  • Anyone looking to make a career change who is a bit lost

User Flow

Base on user stories. I created a user flow with 3 main goals. These goals inlcuded viewing jobs/different articles on a homepage, having a job application checklist, and the ability to send a message

Ideation

In order to get my initial ideas out on paper, I began with the Crazy 8’s method of sketching, in which I sketched eight screens within eight minutes. This was a good method to explore different screen designs as well as user flows, and quickly develop any initial concepts with little effort.

Mid Fidelity Wiredrames

After my brainstorming, I started converting these ideas into Sketch. First I began with what was most familiar to me, which was iOS design guidelines. Since I wanted Gray to also be available for Android users, I had to do some research on Material Design Guidelines, a new concept to me. Key differences between iOS Design and Material Design are mostly Navigation Systems and general styling of individual UI Elements.

Conclusion

When I began working on this project, the iPhone 12 and iOS 14 had just been released. Therefore, there was a bit of a learning curve for me when learning this operating system, and a new iPhone. This called for some adjustment and familiarizing myself with the newest iOS design guidelines.

This project was also a new opportunity for me to learn about Androids and Material Design. As someone who has had an iPhone for as long as they can remember, I did not have a lot of knowledge or exposure to Android apps. However, I quickly found it pretty simple to learn, and it was a matter of giving some of my UI elements more of a “realistic” feel, such as shadows and highlights.
